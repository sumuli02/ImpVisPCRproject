1.3.1 Fluorescence threshold

The amplification curve is shown on the plot below in red. The green horizontal line represents the fluorescence threshold level.

Drag the green point on the vertical axis to vary the fluorescence threshold level.

  • What is the change in the Ct value?
  • What happens when the threshold level is low?
  • What happens when the threshold level is high?


The fluorescence threshold can't be too low as we avoid the fluctuations of the background fluorescence level.
It can't be too high either (e.g. into linear or plateau phases) as sensitivity of the data would be lost and it is difficult to predict and distinguish the Ct values between different samples.

In general we would set the threshold value within the exponential phase, where the fluorescence level of the sample shows a sharp increase.
