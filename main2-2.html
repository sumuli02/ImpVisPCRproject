Section 2: Calculations

2.2 Calculation

2.2.1 Delta-delta Ct method

The delta-delta Ct method, is a simple formula used in order to calculate the relative fold gene expression of samples when performing qPCR. The overall formula to calculate the relative fold gene expression level can be presented as

$2^{-∆∆Ct}$

where Ct is the cycle threshold value.
Meaning of this formula

(i) Double deltas
$∆$ is a mathematical symbol representing the change in a particular variable. Here, there are two deltas. The first delta indicates the change in Ct value of the treated sample relative to the untreated sample. In other words,
∆∆Ct = ∆(Treated sample) - ∆(Untreated sample)

The second delta indicates the change in Ct value of the target gene with respect to the housekeeping gene, i.e.
∆Ct = Ct(Target gene) - Ct(Housekeeping gene)

We calculate the change in Ct value of the two genes in both the treated and untreated samples.
(ii) Power of 2
To deduce conclusions for this experiment, we would like to calculate the fold change in gene expression of your target gene in experimental conditions, relative to control, which has been normalized to your housekeeping gene. This is the ratio between changes in Ct values in the treated sample to that in the untreated.
The power of 2 accounts for the fact that every PCR cycle doubles the amount of the gene. The presence of minus sign in the formula is because Ct value decreases with the rate of gene expression, as illustrated in the previous section.

Procedure of using the formula
  1. Average the Ct values for any technical replicates (called the normalized Ct value).
  2. Calculate the ∆Ct for each sample.
  3. Calculate the average ∆Ct for untreated sample.
  4. Calculate the ∆∆Ct for each sample.
  5. Calculate the fold gene expression values.
